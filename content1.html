What is the meaning of Environmental Protection?

Environmental protection is a way for people to protect the earth's natural habitat from encroachment by individuals, organizations or governments. People can repair and improve the damage to natural resources and the environment by protecting or changing the current trend of the environment.
However, due to continued population growth and overconsumption, especially today's technological pressures, the biophysical environment has become frequently and permanently degraded. Many of the world's environmental organizations have also begun to restrict these activities that lead to environmental degradation. At the same time, pollution levels of air, water, plastics and other natural resources are on the rise, requiring more effective environmental policies in all countries.
Not only that, but another term for environmental protection can also be described as the prevention and protection of unnecessary but present changes in natural ecosystems caused by human actions. The awareness of environmental protection includes stopping the over-exploitation of natural resources, reducing all kinds of pollution and so on. In particular, one of the ways to improve People's Daily awareness of environmental protection is to take measures conducive to environmental protection, such as the three R's waste grade.
The THREE R’s in Waste Hierarchy ACTION!


REDUCE

The concept of reducing production and consumption is crucial to waste management systems. This logic can easily be understood as if there is less waste, then less will be recycled or reused. Therefore, "reduce" means to minimize the amount of waste people produce and throw away.

REUSE

Reuse refers to using the item multiple times. When people find that there are useless or damaged items, we can choose to keep them in case we find other uses for them in the future. In fact, whether it is finding bargains in old furniture or getting repairable items in picking up, people can reuse these items. However, learning to reuse items or repurpose them in different ways in the intended way are all vital behaviors in waste management.

RECYCLE

The final stage of waste classification is the act of recycling. Recycling an item means that it will be converted into raw material again and shaped into a new item instead of throwing it away. However, there are very few materials on earth that cannot be recycled. Therefore, people should understand which products can be recycled or not. Through this careful selection of recyclable product opportunities, this can also be the first step towards efficient recycling.
How to sort all the garbage?

Recyclable Waste

◈ Also called renewable resources, it refers to garbage suitable for recycling in domestic garbage.

◈ Paper (yellow): newspapers, magazines, old books and other uncontaminated paper products.

◈ Glass (Blue): glass containers, flat glass and other glass products.

◈ Plastic (red): Styrofoam, plastic packaging, plastic tableware, and so on.

Hazardous Waste

◈ Garbage that is harmful to human health or the natural environment in life.

◈ It must be handled safely by a professional organization certified in the field of environmental protection.

◈ For example: batteries, old electronic products, expired cosmetics, waste mercury thermometers, paint buckets.

Food Waste

◈ Mainly leftovers produced after meals and waste parts of cooking materials.

◈ Give an example: liquid food residues, wet garbage, waste cooking oil or plant residues and leaves, and so on.

Residual Waste

◈ Other household garbage except recyclables, hazardous garbage and kitchen waste.

◈ This is the main garbage handled by the current sanitation system.

◈ The garbage includes non-recyclable paper, contaminated plastic products, unidentifiable types of garbage, and so on.
